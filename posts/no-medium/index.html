Why I won't write for Medium

opinion 2021-04-29 466 words 3 minutes
Contents

Well its simple I don’t want people to pay 5$ a month just to read my articles. It’s not that I don’t think my writing is not 5$ worth (I believe good writing is almost priceless for the fact that how someone makes you feel with just a bunch of words cannot have a quantifiable amount attached to it) I just want my writing to be freely available.

Now just to be clear I’m not against Medium or its business model and I don’t condemm people who post on medium. This is mostly an opinion I have formed based on my experience with the platform as a Reader.

As someone who is from the tech background Blogs are the go to place for any understanding any new concept or implementing something. A blog is different than stack overflow in the sense stack overflow provides exact syntax for an implementation. Blogs along with source code also provide indepth explanation and are mostly split into multiple chapters covering the same topic in parts. In my opinion blogs are the new reseach papers where approaches to complex problems are explained in detail and just like them they are free of costs!. '

There is nothing better than knowledge being free and easily accesible.

Medium stands in the way of this lofty goal. Now I cannot do anything about Medium’s payment policy or people who publish their knowledge over there. I can however post my learnings and knowledge on some other platform something which is freely available. So I would be doing it here. I hope I am able to maintain this resolve in the future as well.

Allow me to take this opportunity to explain what this platform is going to be about. As mentioned in the about section this is going to be a place where in I would write stuff which I’m interested in. All though they will be generic in nature the blogs in the technology category will be about some of the problems I have worked and solved at job. Some of these problems during the time had little to no solution and that increased the challenge of solving them. Since I was able to come out on the other side of these challenges victories I’m sure a lot of people who are solving similar problems or in the same domain would be stuck or need any kind of guidance in understanding what happens when they implement the solution in production along with the best practices surrounding them. I would utlizise blogs to bring in light great solutions and also open a dialogue as to what could be done better. the idea is simple

“solve problems no is solving, tell everyone about it , engage in a dialogue for more complex problem”

inshort stay tuned.
